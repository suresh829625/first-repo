HAPPY MOTHERS DAY

In today’s fast-paced world, it is easy to take our mothers for granted. Mother’s Day serves as a reminder to slow down and acknowledge her importance. It is also an opportunity to reflect on the role of maternal figures—grandmothers, aunts, teachers—who nurture us with the same care.

Mother’s Day is a day to thank the most important person in our lives—our mother. She is the one who loves us before we even open our eyes, who stays awake when we are sick, and who celebrates our smallest achievements. On this day, children give cards, flowers, or gifts to show their love. But the best gift is our time and affection. A simple hug or a heartfelt “I love you” can mean more than anything bought from a store. Mother’s Day reminds us that our mothers deserve respect and care every day. They are our first friends, our strongest supporters, and our lifelong guides.

Mother’s Day: Honouring the Heart of the Family Mother’s Day is celebrated worldwide to acknowledge the invaluable role of mothers in shaping individuals and societies. In India, as in many other countries, it is observed on the second Sunday of May. While the customs may vary, the sentiment remains the same—gratitude for a mother’s love. From the moment a child is born, a mother’s life changes forever. She becomes a source of comfort, protection, and wisdom. Her sacrifices often go unnoticed—skipping meals so her children can eat, working late into the night to ensure their comfort, or silently enduring hardships to shield them from pain. Mother’s Day is an opportunity to recognise these silent acts of love. Families celebrate by preparing special meals, giving thoughtful gifts, or planning outings. Schools often organise events where children perform songs, dances, or speeches dedicated to their mothers. However, the true spirit of Mother’s Day goes beyond celebration. It is a reminder to value and respect mothers every day. In a world where many women juggle careers, household responsibilities, and caregiving, acknowledging their efforts is essential. Moreover, Mother’s Day is also a time to remember those who have lost their mothers or those whose mothers are far away. Acts of kindness towards other maternal figures—grandmothers, guardians, mentors—can make the day meaningful for everyone. In conclusion, Mother’s Day is not just about gifts or greetings; it is about recognising the foundation of love and care upon which our lives are built.
